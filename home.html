U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)

"Building a Healthier America"

The HHS supports the Health and Wellness of the American people through medical advances/research, human services, and public health.

The HHS's current objectives are to improve health care accessibility and associated costs, expand behavioral health programs, address socioeconomic factors as it relates to healthcare, and effectively manage the COVID-19 pandemic.

There are 12 Agencies under the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. Of these agencies, 9 divisions are part of the U.S. Public Health Service and 3 are part of human services.

    Public Health Service Commissioned Corps (USPHS)

    A uniformed branch within the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services that serves to protect the health of Americans and globally. This division is not part of the Armed Forces. Healthcare professionals who want to become officers and commit their invaluable skills to the Federal Public Health Service branch have extensive opportunities within the USPHS.

    • Active-duty officers are full-time, first-line responders during public health emergencies.
    • Reserve-duty officers are full-time and provide support for active duty officers.
    • They have the ability to grow professionally as well as academically through the USPHS education benefits.
    • Officers are stations across the nation, globally, and in underserved regions protecting the health of others.
